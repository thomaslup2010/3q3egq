Japanese history

  1. Ancient Japan (~300 BCE - 710 CE): The Yamato clan unified parts of Japan and established the emperial dynasty, which still exists today

  2. Classical Period (710 - 1185): Buddhism, Chinese writing, and government systems were introduced, and the first permanent capital was build in Nara

  3. Feudal Era (1185 - 1603): Samurai warriors and shoguns ruled much of Japan, this era was marked by war and shifting power

  4. Edo Period (1603 - 1868): A peacful time under the Tokugawa shogunate, Japan closed itself off from most foreign influence

  5. Meiji Restoration (1686): Japan modernized rapidly and became an industrial and military power

  6. World War II: Japan played a major role and was defeated in 1945 after the atomic bombings of Hiroshima and Nagasaki

  7. Modern Japan: Today, Japan is a peacful, democratic country known for innovation, technology, culture, and a strong economy
